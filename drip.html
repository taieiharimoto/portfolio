Every inch counts

Every inch of water pressure counts for a gravity-fed drip irrigation system. For farmers carrying water manually into the system, low operating pressure means less lifting. Regardless of water source, low operating pressure always means reaching a larger plot area with the pressure at hand. Proximity Designs was a pioneer in bringing drip to Myanmar in 2008, with a locally produced, low-cost system making cutting-edge drip technology attainable for smallholders. In 2013, the product design team embarked on an ambitious redesign of the system, with the goal of making it as efficient as possible.

A Proximity drip irrigation system installed in a farmer's plot in Myanmar's Dry Zone.

Two feet is all you need

The team scrutinized every component of Proximity’s drip system, with a focus on reducing flow resistance and cost. In the end, we succeeded in designing the world’s most efficient* drip irrigation system, requiring only 2ft of head for 1,200 emitters. Not only did this mean less lifting of water into the system, it enabled farmers to simply set up a water tank on a mound of sandbags, instead of requiring a wooden or cement stand structure, significantly reducing the system cost.
*Based on analysis of competitive products at the time.


All of Proximity’s drip system components are manufactured in Myanmar. This reduced costs and afforded production agility, but also significantly increased the challenge of achieving high quality parts with tight tolerances necessary in reducing flow resistance.

Holistic design

A great product is rarely standalone. Instead, great products are integrated with well-designed services such as sales and delivery, installation and after-care. This is especially necessary when introducing a new technology such as drip, which is not always intuitive at first use. Recognizing this, the team took a holistic approach and spent an equal amount of time redesigning service delivery surrounding Proximity’s drip irrigation product. Pushing boundaries of quality

As project lead, I managed all aspects of product design, leading the team through needfinding, prototyping and testing, design for manufacturing, and product launch. I oversaw the service redesign sub-project, recruiting and supporting a service designer and her team. I am proud of the team’s creativity and hard work to push the boundaries of quality and precision for locally produced components, and the many learnings gained from applying design principles in new disciplines like service and communication design.
