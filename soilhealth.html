What's in my soil?

Nobody understands a farmer’s own plot better than the farmers themselves. Yet at the same time, Myanmar paddy farmers are in the dark when it comes to the nutrition content of their soil, simply because tools to measure something so intangible are not made accessible to them. They have no choice but to make critical inputs decisions based on intuition at best, or predatory marketing promises from chemical input companies at worst.

Affordable, accurate, actionble

Seeing this pressing need, Proximity Labs designed a soil nutrition testing service, complete with personalized fertilizer recommendations, that is affordable, accurate, and actionalbe. The team co-created the service together with farmers and Proximity’s Farm Advisory Service (FAS) business unit, making sure it is both effective and efficient. It was FAS’s first fully financially sustainable service, with a value proposition to farmers that well exceeded the service fee (which covered all operating costs). Careful attention was paid to the service design, making sure customer touchpoints maximized trust and actionability, to empower farmers to make inputs decisions based on best possible knowledge of the status of their plots and the downstream effects of input applications.

Service design overseer and technical lead

As overseer of the Soil Health project, I supported the team and the project lead throughout the design process, all the way to launch. I also designed the algorithm for recommendation generation based on factors of season, plot size, soil type, and paddy strain. The Soil Health service was a milestone for the organization, demonstrating that impactful extension services could also be revenue generating. It took a talented team of service and business designers, chemists and agronomists, field staff and customers, to make this service a reality. I am honored to have gotten to guide and oversee this project.
