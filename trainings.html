Teaching design

Beyond leading my own design teams, I have had the privilege of teaching and training groups in design, as well as facilitating design workshops within Proximity and with external groups. Like any design project, teaching and facilitation first begins with understanding needs. In a context like Myanmar, where design as a concept can be counter-cultural in many ways, effective training meant more than translating and communicating concepts. As was the case with the example of Proximity's bootcamp below, actual demonstrations and practice by participants were key.

Together with Proximity’s internal staff training team, I led a Design Thinking Bootcamp in 2017. It was based on IDEO’s Bootcamp material, however, it required almost a complete redesign to meet the needs of Proximity’s staff. Dozens of staff participated over two days and engaged in the methods of design. A focus was placed on application, with a day-long mini design project leading to follow-ups and support to implement prototypes over the next two months.
