About Taiei

Hi!

As a kid I was either riding my bike with my dad, or taking things apart to see how they were made. It was like detective work, uncovering the thoughts of the people who designed these things, and I marveled at their creativity. Respect for the people behind products led me to become a designer and product engineer myself. Finding a well-designed product and appreciating how it is made is still most satisfying for me.

In university I was exposed to the field of design for impact. I was inspired by people from all sorts of fields applying their expertise towards the world’s most pressing problems, and I wanted to do the same. The d.school at Stanford took me to Myanmar to work with Proximity in 2009, and I was hooked. I moved there in 2012 and continued working with Proximity for 7 years. While there I learned a tremendous amount about design and engineering, manufacturing in developing contexts, and leadership.

I also love food and traveling to find new tastes. I like learning about cultures and faiths, and have been deeply moved by Christ’s example of love. I’ve toured on motorcycle in Asia, North America, and Europe, and when I can I still bike with my dad.
