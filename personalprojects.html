Self-centering camera ball head

Sketches of mechanisms for self-centering and locking the ball head.
Exploded view of ball head.
Machining the camera mount flange of the ball head.

Photography is cool, both as an art form and also for its tech, and I love how these two aspects inform each other. In university, I designed a ball head joint to mount between a camera and a tripod or monopod. Especially for use in action photography, I wanted a self-centering feature to quickly orient the camera. The final product was machined out of aluminum, brass, and stainless steel, with 10 hand-machined parts. Many custom fixtures were required for cutting the complex geometries on a lathe and mill.

Hashi

Studying hashi ergonomics.
Cutting prototype hashi.
Machined hashi ready to be removed from blank.

Cooking and eating are passions of mine. Growing up in Japan, to me the most important utensils for these passions are hashi, or chopsticks. I designed and made custom hashi as an expression of both my love of food and making things. The hashi were machined in a CNC mill out of aluminum, with magnetic, stainless steel inserts for arrangement in a magnetic case. Finished in black and clear anodizing, my hashi are lovingly used by me and family members to this day.

Angelo's foot guards

Foot guard release pull detail.
Foot guard locking holes detail.
Oprah and Angelo.

I had the privilege of meeting Angelo in university. A brilliant mechanical engineering student and quadriplegic, Angelo was a joy and inspiration to know. To complete his degree, he took machine shop courses as required, however, he was not able to operate the machines himself. I was asked to work with Angelo in his designs and the actual machining of his class projects. Angelo’s foot guards are tailored to him, with an easy to fix front-approach locking mechanism which guards his feet when navigating through unpredictable crowds. The foot guards are made of machined brass, stainless steel, and machined and bent aluminum.
